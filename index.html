Senior content designer

Department for Education — July 2020 to present


Work across 4 transactional services and smaller policy projects involving teacher recruitment in England.

Manage a team of content designers by providing advice, reviewing work and getting the team to share skills and knowledge. For example – I got content designers to contribute to style guide to help us use consistent language in teacher recruitment.

Collaborate across multidisciplinary teams with product managers, content and interactions designers, user researchers, business analysts and developers. For example – worked with our product manager and technical lead to prioritise content and design work for a large piece of work where we’re changing the process for how people apply for teacher training.

Advocate for a user centred approach in service teams. For example – improved the way people write a ‘personal statement’ for teacher training by mapping the journey and using html prototyping, working with user researchers to test designs and advocating for a simpler solution that met user needs.

Work with policy teams to make sure new policies are aligned to user needs. For example – distilling a 45 page policy document into 4 questions for users offering subject knowledge enhancement courses to people applying to teacher training.

Use content and design patterns from the GOV.UK Design System to make sure content is accessible and consistent. For example – in 6 months redesigned a service built by developers in Microsoft Dynamics into an accessible and usable service to help users convert schools to academies.

Developed a content strategy for a service as it moved from private to public beta to help users convert a school to an academy. This gave content design a ‘voice’ in the service line by highlighting who content designers collaborate with to create and manage content.

Analyse user feedback to iterate and improve content, making sure it’s user-centred, while also following the GOV.UK style guide so content is consistent, accessible and understood. For example – analysed quantitative and qualitative research with a business analyst and user researcher to create an accessible way for users to compare data from one service with another.

Map user journeys and design flows to scope work and provide content solutions at appropriate steps in the user journey. For example – worked with content designers to map email communications we send when users apply for teacher training, this helped refine and remove unnecessary content.

Working with a design lead to understand the journey an international person takes to apply for teacher training in England. This work will lead to improvements across our services and highlight any gaps that content design can support like helping users understand the school and degree grades required to do teacher training.

Record our user centred approach by writing design histories on features of work to share with other teams to advocate for user centred design. For example – wrote a story called ‘When Franics felt frustrated’ to help explain the pain points around a policy that needed improving and shared this with senior stakeholders.

Contribute to the DfE content design community of practice through sharing work and helping others with theirs. For example – created guidance and supported content designers to use the GOV.UK prototyping kit to improve technical html, Nunjucks and GitHub skills in the department.

Design assessor for DfE – assessed 3 government services to make sure they are meeting the 14 government Service Standards.

Content designer

Irwin Mitchell LLP — July 2018 to July 2020


Wrote and managed content for the Irwin Mitchell website focussing on personal and business legal services.

Worked with lawyers to change complex legal jargon into plain English and built up good relationships by showing how content was performing once improved. For example – worked with immigration lawyers to provide new guidance on new visa requirements leading up to Brexit.

Used tools such as Google Analytics and Siteimprove to evaluate content performance and make improvements to the accessibility of our website. For example – making sure html was structured correctly so users could navigate it with a keyboard.

Ensured content follows brand tone of voice and style guidelines across the website by developing the team’s first microcopy style guide to help us write consistent error messages, button copy and page and title names.

Peer reviewed content written by colleagues in the digital marketing team and gave constructive feedback to help improve content.

Lead UX writer on the company’s first agile project (using scrum methodology).

Collaborated with legal experts, UX designers, developers and QA testers to deliver a transactional service – a client portal to help clients manage legal work online.

Evaluated forums and legal websites to gain extra insight into our users to create content using language familiar to them.

Held user testing sessions to constantly test and improve our product for users. Used the insights from this testing to make sure the content in the service was user-centred.

Content creator and event coordinator

PayFast Ltd — August 2016 to March 2018


Created content for the PayFast website, newsletters, blog, print media and Google Adwords.

Used Google Analytics to report on the website’s performance and used the insight to improve the website. For example – publishing a page on the service’s fees after seeing this was the top request our support agents received.

Worked with an interaction designer to improve the journey and messaging when someone makes an online payment. For example – having better error messaging that guided a user to fix the mistake.

Wrote content on e-commerce and fintech options to help small business owners start and grow their business.

Gathered insight from users of PayFast services on social media and attended events to directly connect with them. This helped tailor content to the problems users had with using PayFast.

Carried out PayFast’s social media strategy and set up paid advertising on Facebook, Youtube, Twitter and LinkedIn.

Maintained and monitored PayFast’s online reputation by responding quickly to client queries and connecting with our online audience to find out what they need.

Networked and maintained relationships with different event partners.

Coordinated events and sponsorships and managed the logistics of setting up and taking down exhibition spaces across the country.

Worked with suppliers to produce PayFast branded merchandise for events.
