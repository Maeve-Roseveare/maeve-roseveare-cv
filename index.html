Senior content designer

Department for Education — July 2020 to present


Senior content designer working on a service line called 'Becoming a teacher' which consists of 5 transactional services and various smaller policy projects around teacher recruitment in England.

Work in multidisciplinary teams where I collaborate daily with user researchers, interaction designers, business analysts, developers, product and delivery managers.

Work with our policy design team to make sure new policy developments are aligned to user needs by offering advice and best practice in content design.

Work with wider marketing teams to support their aims in creating email communications to attract people to teacher training.

Use content and design patterns from the GOV.UK Design System to make sure content in my service line is consistent, and aligns with wider government styles.

Analyse user feedback to iterate and improve content, making sure it’s user-centred, while also following the GOV.UK style guide so content is consistent, accessible and understood by users.

Keep track of design and content decisions by writing design histories on features of work so I can share with other teams.

Hold monthly meetups with all content designers in my division to share knowledge, critique work, support each other and more junior content designers.

Line manage 4 content designers and mentored 2 junior content designers.

Developed a content strategy for a service to help users convert a school to an academy.

Map user journeys and design flows to scope work and provide content solutions at appropriate steps in the user journey.

Create functional prototypes using the GOV.UK prototyping kit in HTML and Nunjucks and have trained other content designers to do this too.

Confident in using GitHub and Heroku to collaborate with front-end developers and interaction designers to push code.

Create low fidelity prototypes with quick sketches on paper or with tools like Figma or Lucidboard.

Use quantitative data tools like Google Analytics, Lookerstudio and Blazer to see how users interact with our services and use this insight to improve content.

Contribute to the DfE content design community of practice through sharing work and helping others with theirs.

Have assessed 3 government services as a Design Assessor to make sure services are meeting government Service Standards.
